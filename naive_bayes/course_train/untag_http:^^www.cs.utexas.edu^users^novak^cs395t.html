CS 395T: Automatic Programming CS 395T: Automatic Programming Automatic Programming is the generation of executable programs from specifications that are higher-level than ordinary programming languages. The course will consist of lectures for the first two-thirds of the semester. Homework problems and programming assignments will be given to illustrate the lecture material. The programs will not be long, but will require learning to use several kinds of programming systems. The latter part of the semester will cover readings in the research literature. Students will be expected to present one or two papers to the class.
